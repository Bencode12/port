On the Differential Geometry of Ideal Rocket Trajectories

original research • differential geometry • optimal control

[Riemannian manifold visualization]

A foundational differential-geometric treatment of ideal rocket trajectories under vacuum operation. Constructed the rocket phase manifold $(M,g)$—a Riemannian 2-manifold where the Tsiolkovsky delta-v relation emerges as a geodesic arc-length formula. Proved eight main theorems including: the Arc-Length Theorem identifying $\Delta v$ with $L_g(\gamma)$, the Geodesic Theorem characterizing constant-thrust burns as unique geodesics, the Flatness Theorem proving $R \equiv 0$ and deriving global isometry with Euclidean $\mathbb{R}^2$, and the Conjugate-Point Theorem establishing global optimality. Extended results to Hamiltonian mechanics on the cotangent bundle, symplectic reduction, and multi-stage rocket optimization via fiber-bundle splicing. Complete with rigorous proofs and numerical validation across multiple propellant systems.

Determinant Congruence for Sum-Entry Matrices

original proof • linear algebra

[Matrix visualization]

Proved that for any $n \times n$ matrix $A$ with entries $A_{ij} = i + j$, the determinant satisfies $\det(A) \equiv 0 \pmod{n}$ for all $n \geq 3$, and in fact $\det(A) = 0$ exactly. The proof demonstrates that all columns of $A$ lie in the span of two vectors $u = (1,1,\ldots,1)^T$ and $v = (1,2,\ldots,n)^T$, establishing linear dependence when $n \geq 3$. Verified the result through row operations on small cases and generalized using vector space dimension arguments. Includes explicit computations for $n=1,2$ edge cases where the determinant is non-zero.
